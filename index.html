College Guide

College of Valor

A college focused on combat and inspiration, perfect for bards who enjoy melee combat.

Core Abilities:
  • Combat Inspiration (Level 3)
  • Extra Attack (Level 6)
  • Battle Magic (Level 14)
Best Attributes:
  • Primary: Charisma ★★★★★
  • Secondary: Dexterity ★★★★
  • Constitution: ★★★
Multiclass Options:
  • Fighter (2-3 levels): Action Surge, Fighting Style
  • Paladin (2 levels): Divine Smite, Fighting Style
  • Hexblade (1-3 levels): Weapon proficiencies, CHA for attacks
Gameplay Strategy:
  • Front-line support with medium armor
  • Combine spells with weapon attacks
  • Use Combat Inspiration for team damage boost
College of Lore

Masters of knowledge and magic, excelling in versatility and skill expertise.

Core Abilities:
  • Bonus Proficiencies (Level 3)
  • Cutting Words (Level 3)
  • Additional Magical Secrets (Level 6)
  • Peerless Skill (Level 14)
Best Attributes:
  • Primary: Charisma ★★★★★
  • Secondary: Dexterity ★★★
  • Constitution: ★★★
Multiclass Options:
  • Sorcerer (3 levels): Metamagic, extra spells
  • Warlock (2 levels): Eldritch Blast, Invocations
  • Wizard (2 levels): Arcane Recovery, School benefits
Gameplay Strategy:
  • Stay at range and support with spells
  • Use Cutting Words to prevent enemy success
  • Leverage wide skill proficiency

Bardic Inspiration Guide

Basic Mechanics

Understanding the core mechanics and best timing for Bardic Inspiration.

Basic Rules:
  • Inspiration Die: d6 (d8 at 3rd, d10 at 5th, d12 at 10th)
  • Usage Timing: Before making a check
  • Duration: 10 minutes or until used
  • Gain Method: Through performance, speech, or inspiring actions
Resource Management:
  • Daily uses = Charisma modifier (minimum 1)
  • Recover all uses on short rest
  • Save 1-2 uses for critical moments
  • Prioritize core team members
Combat Applications

Explore optimal usage strategies in combat.

Key Moments:
  • Critical attacks in boss battles
  • Important saving throws
  • Healing checks for dying allies
  • Control-breaking checks
Tactical Coordination:
  • Fighter: Important strike opportunities
  • Wizard: Crucial spell saves
  • Rogue: Sneak attacks or trap disarming
  • Cleric: Critical healing or dispelling
Combat Tips:

Plan inspiration usage at the start of combat. Observe enemy types and battlefield environment to anticipate key moments.

Social Applications

Flexible use of inspiration in social situations.

Social Scenarios:
  • Important negotiations
  • Persuading nobles or officials
  • Gathering key information
  • Performance competitions
Skill Synergy:
  • Persuasion: Diplomatic negotiations
  • Deception: Identity concealment
  • Intimidation: Pressure tactics
  • Performance: Gaining support
Social Tips:

Discuss potential inspiration needs with teammates before important social encounters. Prepare appropriate performance or speech content to justify inspiration grants.

Exploration Applications

Strategic use of inspiration during exploration and adventure.

Exploration Scenarios:
  • Deciphering ancient texts
  • Tracking important clues
  • Navigating dangerous terrain
  • Disarming traps
Skill Applications:
  • Athletics: Climbing, swimming
  • Stealth: Sneaking and scouting
  • Investigation: Searching for clues
  • Survival: Tracking and navigation
Exploration Tips:

Pay attention to environment descriptions during exploration to anticipate potential dangers. Distribute inspiration uses wisely, don't waste them on non-critical checks.

Advanced Techniques

Master advanced applications of Bardic Inspiration.

College Feature Synergy:
  • College of Valor: Combat Inspiration
  • College of Lore: Cutting Words
  • College of Glamour: Mantle of Inspiration
  • College of Swords: Blade Flourish
Spell Synergy:
  • Heroism: Extra temporary HP
  • Greater Invisibility: Enhanced survival
  • Invisibility: Stealth actions
  • Dominate Person: Social assistance
Advanced Tips:

Choose appropriate inspiration usage based on your college features. Stacking with spell effects creates stronger results.

Equipment Guide

Weapon Selection

Explore weapon options suitable for bards.

Recommended Weapons:
  • Shortsword: Light and versatile
  • Longbow: Ranged support
  • Hand Crossbow: Quick shots
  • Flute: Spellcasting focus
Weapon Characteristics:
  • Prioritize light weapons
  • Easy to carry
  • Doesn't interfere with spellcasting
  • Versatile functionality
Weapon Tips:

Choose weapons based on your college. College of Valor and College of Swords can use heavier weapons, while other colleges prefer light weapons.

Armor Selection

Understand armor options suitable for bards.

Recommended Armor:
  • Leather Armor: Light and flexible
  • Studded Leather: Better protection
  • Shield: Additional defense
  • Magic Amulets: Special effects
Armor Characteristics:
  • Doesn't interfere with spellcasting
  • Light and portable
  • Doesn't affect Dexterity
  • Highly practical
Armor Tips:

Prioritize armor that doesn't interfere with spellcasting. If you choose College of Valor, consider medium armor.

Adventuring Gear

Explore practical adventuring equipment choices.

Recommended Equipment:
  • Musical Instrument: Spellcasting focus
  • Healing Potion: Emergency healing
  • Spell Scroll: Extra spells
  • Explorer's Pack: Utility tools
Equipment Characteristics:
  • Versatile functionality
  • Light and portable
  • Highly practical
  • Cost-effective
Equipment Tips:

Choose equipment based on your adventure style. Social characters can carry more performance props, while combat-focused characters prioritize protective gear.

Level-Based Equipment

Equipment recommendations for different level ranges.

Levels 1-4:
  • Basic Equipment: Leather Armor, Shortsword, Flute
  • Recommended Items: Healing Potion, Explorer's Pack
  • Budget: 50-100 gold
Levels 5-10:
  • Advanced Equipment: Studded Leather, +1 Weapon, Magic Instrument
  • Recommended Items: Spell Scroll, Ring of Protection
  • Budget: 500-1000 gold
Levels 11-16:
  • High-Level Equipment: +2 Weapon, Magic Armor, Magic Amulet
  • Recommended Items: Teleportation Scroll, Greater Healing Potion
  • Budget: 2000-5000 gold
Levels 17-20:
  • Legendary Equipment: +3 Weapon, Legendary Armor, Artifacts
  • Recommended Items: Resurrection Scroll, Legendary Magic Items
  • Budget: 10000+ gold

Roleplay Guide

Character Background

Create a unique bard character background.

Background Elements:
  • Musical Heritage: Family or mentor's musical traditions
  • Travel Experience: Stories from journeys
  • Performance Style: Unique way of performing
  • Personal Motivation: Drive for adventure
Personality Traits:
  • Outgoing and Cheerful
  • Witty and Humorous
  • Romantic and Artistic
  • Freedom-seeking
Background Tips:

Design your background story based on your chosen college. For example, a Valor bard might come from a military family, while a Lore bard might be a library scholar.

Performance Techniques

Enhance your character's roleplaying performance skills.

Performance Methods:
  • Improvisation: Create poetry based on scenes
  • Musical Performance: Play instruments
  • Storytelling: Share adventure tales
  • Theatrical Acting: Impersonate different characters
Interaction Techniques:
  • NPC Interaction: Build relationships
  • Team Coordination: Inspire teammates
  • Conflict Resolution: Social skills
  • Atmosphere Creation: Scene setting
Performance Tips:

Prepare some improvisation materials like poems, songs, or story snippets that can be quickly used in key moments.

Character Development

Explore directions for character growth and development.

Personal Growth:
  • Skill Enhancement: Music, Performance, Social
  • Relationship Development: With teammates and NPCs
  • Goal Achievement: Pursuit of personal ambitions
  • Character Evolution: Changes from experiences
Story Hooks:
  • Search for Lost Music
  • Uncover Family Secrets
  • Fulfill Mentor's Legacy
  • Achieve Artistic Dreams
Development Tips:

Discuss your character development plans with your DM to ensure personal storylines integrate well with the main campaign.

Performance Materials

Prepare practical performance materials and techniques.

Improvisation Content:
  • Battle Poetry: Describe combat scenes
  • Praise Songs: Celebrate heroic deeds
  • Satirical Verses: Mock enemies
  • Narrative Poems: Tell adventure stories
Performance Props:
  • Instruments: Flute, Harp, Lute
  • Performance Attire: Elegant costumes
  • Small Props: Magical items
  • Performance Tools: Masks, Wigs
Material Tips:

Prepare some general performance materials like openings, endings, and transition phrases to make performances flow more naturally.
